Prototyping at FabCafe Tokyo

2017-01-23

Why We Create Prototype?

Currently, we are focusing on completing our first Mable prototype. Unfortunately, we have neither enough budget nor collaborators as of yet, so we are thinking of having an opportunity for people to take a look at the actual Mable just so you can get to know us.
Of course, we utilize SNS such as Facebook, Instagram, and this blog to spread our idea; however, we believe that you can see and feel much more through the actual product.
For that reason, we are planning an event. Through such experience, we are hoping to hear your honest opinion on Mable for a better and one and only product.

How We Make It?

We also have no equipment at all for Mable production.
It requires a combination of both certain skills and specific machine tools in order to etch the street line on a tabletop.
Lucky enough, we got a first part covered: a skill set to extract street line data from OpenStreetMap and convert to a laser cutting ready.
For a second part, we are getting help from Fab facility, that is proven that we, creators such as ourselves, can perform such an unconventional processing by emergence of digital fablication, or MIT FabLab.


Français : Fab lab de la cité des sciences de Paris.© Benoît Prieur / Wikimedia Commons / CC BY-SA 4.0

Where Has It Been Made?

This time, we made our Mable’s tabletop at FabCafe Tokyo in Shibuya, one of Fab facilities in our neighbors. This place is so great for various reasons. Since it is a part of cafe, we can have some coffee, or even meals (hot dog and open sandwich looked so good by the way) while we are waiting for a process to be done. Of course, we can also have a meeting at the same time.
In a previous experiment (etching the street line on a tabletop), it took us about 2 hours, but this time, we could reduce down to 1+ hour.
Mable is just amazing as it is by a single tabletop; however, if you put several of them all together, you can simply expand the map area that you can have.
Thus, it was great progress that we could shorten time (and financial cost as well since it’s a per-hour charge) so that we can produce twice as many products by the same cost.


Etching the street lines at FabCafe

What’s Next?

Let us go over a Mable production process here;

  1. Exporting data from OpenStreetMap
  2. Converting data for laser cutting
  3. Processing on a tabletop by laser cutter
  4. Polishing a tabletop surface for enhancing quality
  5. Manufacturing table legs
  6. Assembling a tabletop to legs

So far, we have completed from step 1 - 3. And now we are on step 4 - 6 that could elevate the level of perfection as furniture. Since one of our co-founders is an architect, it is not difficult for finishing up those steps, yet we need to standardize table legs for securing more horizontal expansion of Mable.
Although we’ve done step 1 already, we are considering developing a Web service for more efficiency. It will allow you to confirm the finishing product image with the area of your choice. Also, we are going to do projection mapping on Mable, and the tools are under development as well.
We set a goal to complete all 6 steps plus projection mapping by spring this year, so be excited for an upcoming event that we will hold somewhere in Tokyo. It is going to be FUNtastic.

What is Mable?

2016-12-13

Our project name is ‘Mable’. It is a coined word combined ‘Map’ and ‘Table’.

What will we do?

We will create a table with OpenStreetMap data and was inspired ‘Woodcut Data Visualization’. A top board of Mable is cut with a laser cutter by street vector data. We make this data as Scalable Vector Graphics (SVG) using just D3 and use SVG Crowbar. Because it will be able to open the file in Adobe Illustrator for a laser cutter.

Where will we go?

We will run toward creating the production line for Mable and sharing various local community including OSM mappers. Then people can drink, eat, talk and discuss on table that was lined streets of your city.
In addition, we would like to display a graphic animation with a projection mapping. It may be art combined real lines and virtual lines.

What kind of technology is used?

  • OpenStreetMap’s overpass API (GeoJSON)
  • D3.js
  • SVG Crowbar
  • Laser cutter
  • Portable Ultra Short Throw Projector
  • CSS3 animation
